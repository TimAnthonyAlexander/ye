Our Story

From the North Sea to the Bayou

Feli Schenke brought Cuxhaven to the Crescent City

Feli grew up in Cuxhaven, a small harbor town on Germany's North Sea coast, where her Oma ran a konditorei that filled the streets with the scent of butterkuchen and fresh schwarzwälder. After pastry school in Hamburg and a fateful trip to New Orleans — she fell in love with the city's warmth, rhythm, and utter devotion to food — Feli packed two suitcases, her grandmother's rolling pin, and a binder of handwritten recipes.

Goober's opened in the spring of 2019 on a tree-shaded corner of Magazine Street. Named after her rescue beagle Goober (who still naps under the counter), the shop fuses Old World technique with Southern soul. The praline recipe took 47 attempts. The cakepops come in sixteen rotating flavors. Everything is baked before sunrise, and the coffee is always strong enough to make you smile.

Butter. Sugar. Patience. Love.

The Spread

Everything baked fresh daily, come early — we sell out.

Cakepops

Bite-sized joy on a stick. Red velvet, lemon-raspberry, cookies & cream, salted caramel, and a dozen more. Our signature item — Goober's face is on every box.

Cakes

Whole cakes by order. Schwarzwälder kirsch torte, hummingbird cake, pink champagne, and Feli's famous "Cuxhaven Crumb" — a brown-butter streusel cake that tastes like home.

Cookies

Brown-butter chocolate chip, white-chocolate macadamia, pecan sandies, and our chewy ginger-molasses that locals line up for. Sold by the half-dozen.

Muffins & Cupcakes

Blueberry streusel muffins, double-chocolate cupcakes with swiss meringue, and a banana-bourbon cupcake that nods to our Louisiana home.

Pralines

New Orleans tradition, German precision. Creamy, crunchy, made with Louisiana pecans and a whisper of sea salt. Try the chicory-coffee praline — it'll change you.

Coffee & More

Single-origin pour-overs, café au lait, matcha lattes, cold brew steeped 18 hours, and hot chocolate so thick your spoon stands up. Oat milk on the house.
